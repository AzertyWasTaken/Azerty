Basic operations

Home Summary

The 4 basic operations are addition, subtraction, multiplication and division.

Addition

Addition combine two numbers to form a third number, called their sum. This operation is commutative.

Additions are written in the form $n+m$.

The identity of addition is 0. In other words, it means than adding 0 to any numbers do not change it.

Subtraction

Subtraction involves decreasing a numbers by another number. It is the inverse operation of addition.

The resulting number is called the difference. This operation is not commutative.

Subtractions are written in the form $n-m$.

The identity of subtraction is 0.

Multiplication

Multiplication combine two numbers to form a third number, called their product. This operation is commutative.

Multiplications are written in the form $n\times m$.

The identity of multiplication is 1.

Division

Division involves dividing a quantity into equal parts. It is the inverse operation of multiplication.

The resulting number is called the fraction. This operation is not commutative.

Divisions are written in the form $n\div m$.

If a number $n$ if divisible by $k$ if $n\div k$ is an integer.

The identity of division is 1.
