Number

Home Summary

Definition

Numbers are abstract entities used to express quantities, sort items or measure lengths.

They are the basis of mathematics and come in several types, each with its specific properties and uses.

Whole numbers

Whole numbers, also called integers, are numbers used to count.

$...,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,...$

Integers above 0 are called positive numbers while integers below 0 are called negative numbers.

0 is the only number that is neither positive or negative.

Rational numbers

Rational numbers are fractions $a/b$ where $a$ and $b$ are nonzero integers.

Operations

An operation is a process that take two numbers or more and return a single number.

An operation is commutative if changing the order of numbers do not alter the result.

Notation

Numbers are expressed by using numeral systems.

Numeral systems in a base $n$ use $n$ digits.

Numbers represented in base 2 are called binary numbers.
